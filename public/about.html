Discover Arunachal Pradesh

The Land of the Rising Sun

Tawang Monastery

The Tawang Monastery, one of the largest in India, is an iconic landmark of Arunachal Pradesh. Nestled amidst the Himalayas at 10,000 feet, it was founded in the 17th century and serves as a spiritual center for Mahayana Buddhism. Its scenic beauty and cultural significance attract tourists worldwide.

Ziro Valley

Ziro Valley, home to the Apatani tribe, is famous for its rice fields, pine-clad hills, and the UNESCO World Heritage Site nomination. It is also well-known for the Ziro Music Festival, attracting musicians and travelers from across the globe.

Namdapha National Park

Namdapha National Park, one of the largest protected areas in India, is a biodiversity hotspot. From snow leopards to red pandas, it shelters unique flora and fauna. Dense forests and scenic landscapes make it a paradise for wildlife lovers.

Itanagar

Itanagar, the capital of Arunachal Pradesh, is known for its rich culture, beautiful landscapes, and historical significance. It serves as the gateway to the state.

Gompa Temple

Gompa Temple in Itanagar is a serene Buddhist monastery reflecting spirituality, culture, and peace with its stunning architecture and calm surroundings.

Ita Fort

Ita Fort, built in the 14th–15th century, is a historic structure from which Itanagar derives its name. Its stone architecture showcases the ancient heritage of the region.

Indira Gandhi Park

Indira Gandhi Park is a green recreational hub in Itanagar, perfect for peaceful walks, cultural events, and relaxation amidst nature.

Anini Town

Anini, located in Dibang Valley, is known for its untouched beauty, rolling green hills, and pleasant weather. It is a hidden gem of Arunachal Pradesh.

Mechuka

Mechuka Valley is a breathtaking destination surrounded by snow-capped mountains, Buddhist monasteries, and adventure opportunities, often called Arunachal’s hidden paradise.
