Discover Arunachal Pradesh

The Land of the Rising Sun

Tawang Monastery

The Tawang Monastery, one of the largest in India, is an iconic landmark of Arunachal Pradesh. Nestled amidst the Himalayas at 10,000 feet, it was founded in the 17th century and serves as a spiritual center for Mahayana Buddhism.

Ziro Valley

Ziro Valley, home to the Apatani tribe, is famous for its rice fields, pine-clad hills, and the UNESCO World Heritage Site nomination.

Namdapha National Park

Namdapha National Park, one of the largest protected areas in India, shelters unique flora and fauna like snow leopards and red pandas.

Itanagar

Itanagar, the capital of Arunachal Pradesh, is known for its rich culture, landscapes, and historical significance.

Gompa Temple

Gompa Temple is a serene Buddhist monastery reflecting spirituality, culture, and peace with its calm surroundings.

Ita Fort

Ita Fort, built in the 14th–15th century, showcases the ancient stone architecture of the region.

Indira Gandhi Park

Indira Gandhi Park is a peaceful hub for cultural events and relaxation amidst greenery.

Anini Town

Anini, in Dibang Valley, is known for its untouched beauty and pleasant weather.

Mechuka

Mechuka Valley is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and Buddhist monasteries, often called Arunachal’s hidden paradise.
